SEB Partnership

Salisbury's main business organisations, the Chamber of Commerce, Salisbury BID and Sarum Rotary, have come together with all the local secondary schools and colleges to create the Salisbury Education and Business Partnership.

The Partnership provides the opportunity for young people in the city to interact with local businesses and learn about the opportunities available to them. Businesses can get involved by helping with presentations, mock interviews, career fairs and work experience. It's an opportunity to help young people as they make key decisions about their future, and to present positive business role models. The Partnership always welcomes new businesses and individuals with business experience. To join up complete the form here.

Helping Businesses

Supplytrain is a Social Enterprise that helps small businesses recruit and develop young talent through apprenticeships and other government funded training schemes. It's a not-for-profit organisation that invests any surplus funds in community projects, such as this website, that help young people get into good jobs with high quality training.

Maggie Fowler

Maggie Fowler is the founder and a director of Supplytrain. She has had a successful career as an accountant working in sectors from engineering to education which all started with a good employer that offered her day release to take accountancy exams. She has lived in Salisbury for over 20 years and in retirement she has been working with the Chamber of Commerce as their Apprenticeship Champion. She has set up Apprenticeships in Salisbury to support the Salisbury Education and Business Partnership. You can get in touch with Maggie here.

Apprenticeship Ambassador Network (AAN)

Apprenticeships in other Market Towns

This website works well for a market town with a population of up to 100,000. Salisbury doesn't have its own university or a local government centre where an Apprenticeship or Careers Hub might be found in a larger city. But like most market towns it does have well established, effective business groups and the schools come together to discuss and collaborate on activities such as careers education. These networks are the foundation of this website. If you live or work in a similar town and would like help in establishing your own 'Apprenticeships in..' website, get in touch and we will try to help.
