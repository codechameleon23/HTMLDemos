Business Listing Questionnaire

Part 1 The information in this section will be published on the website.

1. Please write a Short Introduction (Required)

Tell us something about your company and the job roles for potential apprentices. The first 35 words will appear on the summary listing with a 'more details' button that will lead to the full summary and the rest of the information.

2. What apprenticeships are you likely to offer in 2022? (Required)

We understand that this won't be set in stone - we're not asking for a commitment - only what your expectations are. We'd like to know the level and the Career Route (As per the apprenticeship standard) This information is aimed at young people who are considering their options on leaving school or college. Some of them will be thinking about taking up an apprenticeship straight away, but we also want to engage with the ones that are thinking about leaving Salisbury to take up a place at university - we need them to understand that there are good jobs in the Salisbury area that they can return to.

3. Do you have plans to develop your apprenticeships in the future? (Optional)

We're interested to know if you have plans to broaden your offer, for example, introducing degree apprenticeships. The website will also be used a careers education resource with students in years 9 and 10 who won't be starting work for at least 2 years.

4. What will be the arrangements for training? (Optional)

Are you an Employer provider? Will the training normally be on site? If not will the apprentice have to travel far? Mention any aspects of training that might be of interest - community projects, competitions etc.

5. Is it easy to get to work? (Optional)

We're aware that this can be a real obstacle for young people starting work. Unless they have parental support for driving lessons and the use of a car, they are unlikely to be driving. So say if your premises are close to public transport links and whether you support other initiatives such as cycle to work, car sharing, lifts to work etc. Do you help with the cost of apprentices travelling to work or to training providers?

6. Do you have a regular recruitment cycle? (Optional)

How many apprentices do you normally take each year? For example, an annual intake each September - for which you start recruiting in February. Young people need to know when to start looking for vacancy adverts.

7. Do you have a profile of an existing apprentice? (Optional)

(if possible, one who went to school locally) that we could use to highlight your apprenticeships in a blog post?

8. Any other information (Optional)

Any other information that you think would be helpful in promoting your apprenticeships. It's worth mentioning part-time or holiday work opportunities, other training schemes you're involved in such as graduate entry and any awards you've won for training.

Part 2 The information in this section will not be published on the website.

How we will use this information.

Part 1 BUSINESS DETAILS AND THE INFORMATION PROVIDED IN Questions 1 and 2 will published on the website. The answers provided in Questions 3 to 7 are to provide Apprenticeships in Salisbury with additional information that may be published, as part of the Business Listing or separately as a Company Profile.

Part 2 The information provided in Part 2 will not be published on the website, but your contact details will be added the Salisbury Education and Business (SEB) Partnership Database and will be made available to members of the Partnership Working Group which comprises the Careers Leads in the Schools which are involved in the Partnership. These individuals may contact you for more information on the apprenticeships you are offering. You can find more details about the SEB partnership here including how to get involved with helping schools with their careers advice, and how to unsubscribe from the database.

Thank you
