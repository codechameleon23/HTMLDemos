What we do

Autonomous Robotics is developing an autonomous underwater vehicle solution – Flying Nodes – to be deployed in swarms of up to 3,000 for assessing and measuring key data in depths of up to 3000 metres.

This ground breaking technology will dramatically reduce the cost of obtaining accurate data in the key sectors of environmental, offshore energy and defence. The Flying Node technology will disrupt these markets by offering commercial advantages in cost, time and/or higher data quality compared to existing technologies.

Autonomous Robotics Ltd (ARL) was set up in 2013 to acquire the assets and Intellectual Property from GoScience Ltd. Goscience had spent a number of years developing an autonomous underwater vehicle (AUV) for the seismic market and had been backed with funding from Shell.

AUV Technology

ARL undertook a detailed review of the system concept including requirements as detailed by Shell together with a feasibility review by QinetiQ and seismic consultants.

The AUV was substantially redesigned and a prototype built in 2017. Proof on concept was determined in 2019 after successfully flying the node underwater and after successfully performing a seismic trial in Portland harbour.

Thalassa

ARL is a wholly owned subsidiary of Thalassa Holdings which is a company listed on the UK and Luxembourg stock exchanges.

Thalassa is an investment group of companies with a wide range of business interests. In 2019 it successfully sold the assets of the WGP Group, a marine geophysical services company, to Magseis Fairfield.
